    Age, Biography and Wiki

    Francys Arsentiev was an American mountaineer who was the first American woman to summit Mount Everest without supplemental oxygen. She was born in Honolulu, Hawaii, and grew up in California. She attended the University of California, Berkeley, where she earned a degree in psychology. In 1988, she and her husband, Sergei Arsentiev, attempted to summit Mount Everest. They were successful, but Sergei died on the descent. Francys was stranded on the mountain for two days before being rescued by a team of Sherpas. In 1998, she returned to Everest and became the first American woman to summit the mountain without supplemental oxygen. She died on the descent, but her body was not recovered until 2005. Francys Arsentiev was an inspiration to many and her legacy lives on in the mountaineering community. She was posthumously awarded the American Alpine Club's David A. Sowles Memorial Award in 2006.
